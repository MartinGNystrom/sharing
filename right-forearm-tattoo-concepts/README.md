# Right Forearm Tattoo Concepts — v1

This folder contains a tattoo-adapted, color-forward graphic-novel tattoo direction for a right-forearm wrap, plus the integrated concept and six exploratory studies that informed it. It is a concept exploration, not a stencil or final tattoo specification.

Open the [visual design guide](index.html) to see the unified composition and the source symbols it integrates.

## Design brief

- Purpose: a personal, symbolic memory jog that can be explained to others.
- Emotional tone: contemplation and resilience.
- Literary anchors: R. S. Thomas's *The Bright Field*, Shakespeare's Sonnet 116, and Kipling's *If*.
- Placement: a full forearm wrap that stops at the wrist and may extend toward the bicep.
- Visual language: original colorful comic-book graphic work, bold clear linework, and non-literal symbolism.
- Constraints: avoid common tattoo imagery and highly recognizable symbols; use words sparingly; include one subtle memento-mori skull.

## Six directions

1. **The Bright Opening** — a hidden radiant field revealed through a fractured dark frame.
2. **The Steadfast Crossing** — a bridge joining a stable tower to a dissolving one through a storm.
3. **Private Cosmology** — an invented seed-form compass with orbiting fragments and a strong graphic silhouette.
4. **Strata and Renewal** — a subtle skull within geological layers, with an unexpected flowering ascent.
5. **Light Through the Storm** — fractured panes turning weather into a colorful, abstract signal of constancy.
6. **The Composed Ascent** — an impossible stairway moving through disruption into a luminous horizon.

The next iteration should develop the strongest one or two directions as a flattened wrap layout, with deliberate inside/outside forearm transitions, before a tattoo artist prepares a body-specific stencil.
